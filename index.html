‚ö†Ô∏è General Notes from Discussion:
  • Scoops: Get "boomy" and muddy at high BPM. Great for reggae, dub, and dubstep, but not ideal for fast genres like jungle, hardtek, or gabber.
  • Horn vs. Ported Trade-offs: Horns offer higher output but come with compromises in complexity, size, phase response, and cost. Modern drivers have reduced the advantage horns once had.
  • Driver Recommendations: B&C 21DS115 and B&C 18DS115-4 frequently mentioned as quality choices.
  • Build Considerations: Track saws greatly improve accuracy for angled cuts. Templates and polyurethane expanding glue help with assembly tolerances.
  • Music Genre Considerations: Hardtek and gabber favor folded horns, while bass-heavy genres like dub can work well with properly tuned ported designs.
